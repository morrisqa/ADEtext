Consider the differential equation

\begin{equation} \left\{ \begin{array}{l} \vec{x}^\prime = \vec{f}(t,\vec{x}) \\ \vec{x}(\alpha)=\vec{\beta} \end{array} \right.\tag{1.2.1} \end{equation}

where \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) \(\vec{\beta}\in \mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) and \(\vec{f}:D \to \mathbb{R}^n\) is a continuous function defined on an open set \(D \subset \mathbb{R}^{n+1}\) containing \((\alpha,\vec{\beta})\text{.}\)
