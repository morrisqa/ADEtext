Theorem 1.2.4. Gronwall's Inequality.

Let \(f:[a,b] \to \mathbb{R} \) be a non-negative function, and let there exist constants \(A,B \ge 0\) such that \(f\) satisfies

\begin{equation*} f(t) \le A + B \int_a^t f(s) \; ds \end{equation*}

for all \(t \in [a,b]\text{.}\) Then \(f(t) \le A e^{B(t-a)}\) for all \(t \in [a,b]\text{.}\)
