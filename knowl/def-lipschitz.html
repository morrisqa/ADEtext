Definition 1.2.5.

A function \(f:I \times \mathbb{R} \to\mathbb{R}^n\) is said to satisfy a Lipschitz condition with respect to \(x\) on the open set \(I \times R \subset \mathbb{R}^2\) if, for any rectangle

\begin{equation*} \Omega:=\{(t,x) \mid |t-t_0|\le a, \; \|x-x_0\| \le b \} \subset I \times \mathbb{R}, \end{equation*}

there exists a constant \(K\) (depending on \(\Omega\)) such that

\begin{equation*} \left|f(t,x)-f(t,y)\right|\le K|x-y| \end{equation*}

for all \((t,x),(t,y) \in \Omega\text{.}\)
