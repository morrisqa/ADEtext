Let \(x_1(t)=x(t)\) and \(x_2(t)=x'(t)\text{.}\) Then \(x_1'(t)=x'(t)=x_2(t)\) and \(x_2'(t)=x''(t)=-x'(t) \cdot x(t) = x_2(t)\cdot x_1(t)\text{.}\) Hence, we can rewrite the second-order differential equation as

\begin{equation*} \left\{ \begin{array}{l} x_1'(t)=x_2(t) \\ x_2'(t)=x_2(t)\cdot x_1(t) \end{array} \right. \end{equation*}
