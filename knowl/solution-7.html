Solution 1.2.7.1.

This example is a bit trickier than ExampleÂ 1.2.6 because our constant \(K\) will depend on \(\Omega\) in this case. Let

\begin{equation*} \Omega:=\{(t,x) \mid |t-t_0|\le a, \; \|x-x_0\| \le b \} \subset I \times \mathbb{R}. \end{equation*}

Then, for all \((t,y) \in \Omega\)

\begin{equation*} \left|f(t,x)-f(t,y)\right| = \left| t^2x^3-t^2y^3 \right| =|t^2| |x^3-y^3|=|t^2||3\xi ^2| |x-y|. \end{equation*}

for some \(\xi \in (x_0-b,x_0+b)\) by the Mean Value Theorem. Let \(A=max\{|t_0-a|,|t_0+a|\}\) and let \(B=max\{|x_0-b|,|x_0+b|\}\text{.}\) Then,

\begin{equation*} \left|f(t,x)-f(t,y)\right| = |t^2||3\xi ^2| |x-y| \le A^2\cdot 3B^2 |x-y|, \end{equation*}

for all \((t,y)\in\Omega\text{.}\) Hence, \(f(t,y)\) satisfies a Lipschitz condition.
